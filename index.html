Permitted Development Rights

This is a bit technical and a bit boring but stay with us.

Changes to Permitted Development (PD) Rights introduced in early April 2014 have transformed the landscape for anyone wishing to undertake a barn conversion in England.

Further amendments took effect on 6 April 2018, including the allowance of up to five new homes to be created from existing agricultural buildings rather than the previous maximum of three.

As a result of the amendment, it will now be possible to convert existing agricultural buildings: barns being one example, into homes without needing to expressly apply for planning permission, as had previously been the case.

The total floorspace of the barn to be converted must be no more than 465m² if its larger you will only be able to convert to a maximum of 465m²*
